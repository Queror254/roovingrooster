Our Services

Roof Maintenance and inspection

Roof maintenance is vital to the longevity of any RV. Inspections should be done every three to six months. It’s much cheaper to replace a skylight when you notice small cracks forming than it is to replace a section of the roof. It’s also a good time to inspect all the seams and touch up any lap sealant that’s needed. Lap sealant will also need to be reapplied every twelve to eighteen months. After three applications of lap sealant it is recommended that the old lap sealant be removed and new lap sealant applied. Inspections price $95. Small areas of lap sealant will be reapplied. Recommend every 3 months Inspection and reapplication $150-$300. Depending on the size of the RV. All seams will have lap sealant reapplied. Recommend every twelve to eighteen months. Inspection and replacement $150-$300+ depending on the size of the RV. Lap sealant degrades over time. Exposure to the sun and elements accelerates that degradation. It’s important to remember that a small leak can lead to $10,000+ in damages before you can see anything from the inside of your RV.

AC maintenance

AC maintenance is impossible to keeping the inside of your comfortable. It’s also important for the longevity of your RV. Cleaning AC coils $95. Keeping the coils clean helps you AC last longer by reducing the workload on the cooling system it’s self. Clean coils work more efficiently and will cool the RV faster while keeping you cooler on exceptionally hot days. AC components inspected $95. Components go bad over time. It’s important that all the AC components are in good working order. A cheap $20 part going out can put a strain on the more expensive motor and compressor. Most motors can be replaced for $300-$500 but if the compressor goes bad then the entire AC will need to be replaced. That can cost from $1800-$2400+. Recommend AC inspection every six to twelve months.

Furnace inspection $95

Furnaces,like the AC, is important to keeping the comfort inside your RV during the cold months. A failure in the furnace can also be a safety issue. Cracks in the heat exchanger can lead to carbon monoxide getting into your rv. All of the safety fears on your furnace are designed to prevent the furnace from over heating. That is why I recommend checking carbon monoxide detector regularly and replacement it every five to seven years and as needed due to unexpected failures. Secondary to the safety issues is the comfort that the furnace gives you during the cold months. Every component on the furnace is designed for safety before comfort and any one of those components can stop your furnace from working. With our training here at Roving Rooster RV maintenance and repair will diagnose whatever is wrong with your furnace and get you living in comfort again.

Water heater inspection $95

Water heater inspection $95. The water heater is similar to the furnace in that is will have overheating protection built in. Any one of the components designed to prevent over heating. Finding these problems before they shut down you water heater is key to keeping the hot water flowing in your RV. Water heater maintenance $95-$200. Water heater maintenance is greatly dependent on your location. If your frequently in an area with hard water then you will have more scale build up. If your water heater is taking longer to heat your water than it used to then you may have scale building up in your hot water tank. Fortunately we can have your water heater quickly making hot water again in just a few hours.
